2026 Update

The CROW has updated its AI usage policy for 2026.

  • All work submitted to The CROW must be student writing.
  • We seek to minimize rejecting student writing, and AI-generated work is no exception. As part of our pursuit of uplifting and improving student writing, we will judge all submitted work similarly.
  • If we detect use of AI in submitted work, authors will receive feedback and guidance on changing AI-generated portions to be in-line with the rest of the essay, and of the level we expect of all work that we publish. This is because, as of now, AI-generated work has distinct weaknesses and flaws that we believe human authors can avoid.
  • We emphatically encourage students to submit work that is wholly written by them, with the understanding that we will be collaborating with authors to polish their work and help them best express their own ideas.

Not at all! CROW stands for Campus Research & Observational Writings. The journal covers all disciplines — science, technology, health, the arts, humanities, and everything in between. If it's research conducted by a UWB student, it belongs here.

Undergraduate and graduate students from all schools and programs at the University of Washington Bothell are eligible to submit. Submissions may also have multiple authors, as long as all authors are UWB students.

The CROW accepts a wide range of research-related work. Accepted submission types include:

  • Research Papers
  • Research Abstracts
  • Research Editorials
  • Researched Argumentative Essays
  • Researched Literary Analyses
  • Research Proposals
  • Literature Reviews
  • Works in Progress

See the Guidelines page for definitions and formatting requirements for each type.

Yes! Ongoing research can be submitted as a Work in Progress (WIP). A WIP should include a hypothesis and methodology, and may include preliminary results if available — final conclusions are not required. Submitting a WIP does not prevent you from publishing the completed paper elsewhere later.

Yes, multi-author submissions are welcome. All listed authors should be UWB students. Each author's name and program will be included in the published work.

Submissions are reviewed by our student editorial board. If your work is selected, you'll enter a collaborative editing process with our editors to refine and polish your writing. Accepted papers may be returned for revision before final publication. Once published, your work will be ineligible for submission to other peer-reviewed journals.

Reach out to us directly at publish@uw.edu — we're happy to help with any questions about submissions, eligibility, or the publication process.

The CROW is an equal opportunity student publication that does not discriminate or publish works that discriminate against any sex, gender expression, sexual orientation, race, color, ethnicity, creed, religion, or ability. We thoughtfully consider all potential publications within this guideline.
